Welcome to Unofficial Website of IPL

The Indian Premier League (IPL) is one of the most celebrated and widely watched cricket tournaments in the world. Launched in 2008 by the Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI), the IPL has transformed cricket into a high-octane sport that blends entertainment with the spirit of competition.

History and Evolution

The IPL was conceived as a franchise-based Twenty20 (T20) cricket league, featuring teams representing various cities in India. The tournament quickly gained popularity, attracting top cricketing talents from around the globe. The leagueâ€™s unique format, where teams bid for players in a high-profile auction, has made it a lucrative platform for cricketers and a source of endless excitement for fans.

Foundation

On 13 September 2007, following India's victory at the 2007 T20 World Cup, the BCCI announced the launch of the Indian Premier League (IPL), a franchise-based Twenty20 cricket competition. The inaugural season was scheduled for April 2008, starting with a ceremony in New Delhi. BCCI Vice-president Lalit Modi, who led the IPL initiative, outlined the tournament's format, prize money, and squad composition rules.

Expansions and Terminations

Before the fourth season in 2011, Pune Warriors India and Kochi Tuskers Kerala joined the IPL. The Pune franchise was purchased by the Sahara Adventure Sports Group for $370 million, while Rendezvous Sports World acquired the Kochi franchise for $333.3 million. Kochi was terminated after one season due to non-payment of the franchise fee.
